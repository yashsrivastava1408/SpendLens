# Economics

This document outlines the spreadsheet math behind SpendLens as a lead-generation asset for Credex.

## Lead Value Estimate
- **Average Customer Value (ACV) for Credex**: Assuming a company spends $2,000/mo on AI tools, a 30% discount saves them $600/mo. If Credex takes a 15% cut of the savings, that's $90/mo or ~$1,000/yr per customer.
- **Conversion Rate (Lead → Customer)**: 5% of captured leads convert to paying Credex customers.
- **Value per Lead**: $1,000 * 0.05 = **$50 per qualified lead**.

## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) per Channel
- **Organic Social (Twitter/LinkedIn)**: $0 CAC. High time investment, high variance.
- **Hacker News / Communities**: $0 CAC. Spiky traffic.
- **Cold Outbound**: Assuming a tool like Apollo costs $100/mo, and yields 10 leads/month = $10 CAC.
- **Paid Search (e.g., "GitHub Copilot pricing")**: ~$2.50 CPC. At a 10% form conversion and 30% email capture rate (3% overall), CAC = ~$83. (Paid search is currently unprofitable without increasing the conversion rate).

## The Conversion Funnel
1. **Traffic**: 10,000 visitors
2. **Audit Completed**: 3,000 (30%)
3. **Email Lead Captured**: 1,000 (33% of completed audits)
4. **Consultation Call Booked**: 150 (15% of leads)
5. **Credex Credits Purchased**: 50 (33% close rate from calls)

## The Path to $1M ARR
To reach $1,000,000 ARR for Credex (based solely on leads generated by this tool):
- Need 1,000 paying customers (at $1k ACV)
- Funnel requires 20,000 total leads.
- At a 10% overall site-to-lead conversion rate, SpendLens needs **200,000 unique visitors**.
- **Strategy**: Instead of driving 200k raw visitors, we focus on moving upmarket. If we audit Series C companies spending $10k/mo on AI tools, the ACV jumps to $5,000/yr, meaning we only need 200 customers (4,000 leads).
