# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target User
- **Job Title**: CTOs, VPs of Engineering, and technical Founders.
- **Company Stage**: Series A to Series B SaaS startups (20-150 employees).
- **Pain Point**: The company grew fast, gave developers corporate cards, and now has a highly fragmented AI tool stack (paying for Copilot, ChatGPT Plus, and Cursor concurrently).

## Search Intent (What they Google)
- "GitHub Copilot vs Cursor cost for teams"
- "How to manage ChatGPT enterprise licenses"
- "Reduce SaaS sprawl"
- "Average AI tool spend per developer"

## 100-User Plan
1. **Direct Outreach**: Search LinkedIn for "VP Engineering" at startups that recently raised a Series A. Send a hyper-specific message: *"Saw you just scaled to 50 engineers. If you're paying retail for Copilot + ChatGPT, you're likely bleeding ~$15k/yr in redundant AI spend. I built a free 60s audit tool to check: spendlens.vercel.app"*
2. **"Unfair" Channel**: Niche Slack/Discord communities like *Eng Managers Slack*, *CTO Craft*, and specific YC alumni channels. Post the tool as a helpful resource on "Vendor Management Thursdays."

## The Viral Loop
The results page strips PII and creates an `/audit/[id]` URL with dynamic Open Graph images displaying "I'm saving $X/mo on AI tools." Technical founders love to brag about operational efficiency on Twitter.

## Week-1 Traction Metrics (Simulated)
- 400 Unique Visitors (mostly from a Hacker News "Show HN" post)
- 120 Completed Audits (30% conversion rate on the form)
- 45 Leads Captured (37% of completed audits entered an email to see the PDF/detailed report)
- 8 High-Intent Leads (Savings >$500/mo, clicked the Credex CTA)
